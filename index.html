Why Knee Pain Needs a Root-Cause Approach

Many people experience knee discomfort due to overuse, stiffness, inflammation, or age-related wear. Ayurveda focuses on balancing the body and supporting long-term joint function through personalized care.

Ayurvedic Perspective on Knee Pain

In Ayurveda, joint pain is often linked with an imbalance of Vata dosha, which can increase dryness and stiffness. A structured plan usually combines therapies, herbs, and lifestyle recommendations based on the individual.

  • Stiffness after rest or waking up
  • Swelling and tenderness around the joint
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Discomfort during walking or climbing stairs
“Consistency matters more than intensity — gentle care over time supports better mobility.”

Common Therapies Used in Ayurveda

Therapies may differ based on diagnosis and condition severity. Many programs combine external treatments and internal support.

Abhyanga

Medicated oil massage to support circulation and joint comfort.

Janu Basti

Localized oil pooling on the knee area to support lubrication and relief.

Kizhi

Herbal poultice therapy used for stiffness and supportive warmth.

Panchakarma

Detox-based approach that may be recommended in some cases.

Diet & Lifestyle Tips

Small daily habits can make a big difference. Always choose advice suited to the individual and condition severity.

Warm, simple meals

Prefer warm foods and adequate hydration to support comfort.

Gentle movement

Daily walking, stretching, or yoga under guidance can help.

Consistent sleep

Stable sleep routine supports recovery and overall wellness.
